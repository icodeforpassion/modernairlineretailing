Offer & Order journeys

Journey walkthrough: PNR / Ticket world vs Order world

Use the toggle to revisit the original comparison tool showing how a customer journey flows in the legacy stack versus a modern order-native architecture.

Legacy journey flow

  1. Shop: Availability + fare quotes pulled from PSS and ATPCO; product limited to filed combinations.
  2. Price & hold: PNR shell created with time limit; ancillaries added via SSR codes or EMD offers.
  3. Pay: Ticketing service issues e-ticket + EMD, reconciled against PNR and accounting systems.
  4. Service: Change flows reissue tickets, revalidate coupons and trigger queue-based handling.
  5. Settle: BSP/ARC and interline settlement consume coupon-level data; finance reconciles manually.

Artefacts: PNR, e-ticket, EMD, SSR, RBD mappings. Ownership split across distribution, RM and airport teams.

Offer & Order journey flow

  1. Construct offer: Offer management assembles products from dynamic catalogs with attribute pricing and context triggers.
  2. Commit: Order manager persists a single order record with services, payments and fulfilment commitments.
  3. Service: One order spine drives self-service, assisted and partner flows with event-based updates.
  4. Operate: Downstream ops use order events for departure control, disruption handling and partner delivery.
  5. Settle & learn: Clearing, finance and telemetry consume the same order data contract; insights feed back into offer decisioning.

Artefacts: Offer, Order, Service list, Fulfilment record. Ownership sits with cross-functional retail squads anchored by product ops.
